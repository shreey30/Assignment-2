Proportion of domestic students who enrol into undergraduate programs within 3 years of their year 12

Key Insights

This chart shows the trend of students moving directly from high school to higher education. Both immediate and delayed entry rates are increasing, indicating a growing overall participation in higher education. The gap between the two lines (immediate vs total within three years) shows that a significant share of students delay entry â€” but the gap remains fairly stable, suggesting consistent patterns of delayed enrolment. The slight drop around 2020-2021 likely reflects external disruptions (pandemic, economic uncertainty), but the overall trend remains upward.

Between the 2017 and 2023 Year 12 cohorts, the proportion of students commencing undergraduate study immediately after school rose from 36.7% to 42.9%, while the share commencing within three years increased from 45.5% (2017) to 50.9% (2021). Although data for the 2022 and 2023 cohorts are incomplete, projections based on past patterns suggest the 2023 cohort will reach around 52.8% by 2026. A minor shift occurred for the 2020 cohort, where more students entered university directly after Year 12, likely due to reduced gap-year opportunities during the COVID-19 pandemic.
